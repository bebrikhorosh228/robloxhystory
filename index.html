In March 2007, Roblox became compatible with COPPA, with the addition of secure chat, which limited users under the age of thirteen to communicate by selecting predefined messages from the menu. In August, Roblox developers added a Builders Club, a premium subscription, and improved servers. In December 2011, Roblox hosted its first Hack Week, an annual event where Roblox developers work on innovative, non-standard ideas for new developments to be presented to the company. On December 11, 2012, Roblox released a version of the game platform for iOS. On October 1, 2013, Roblox released the Developer Exchange system, which allows developers to exchange Robux earned from their games for real currency. On May 31, 2015, a feature was added that increases the graphical accuracy of the terrain and changes the physics engine from a block-oriented style to a smoother and more realistic style. On November 20, Roblox was launched on Xbox One with an initial selection of 15 games selected by Roblox staff. New Roblox games for this console must go through the approval process and meet the standards of the Entertainment Software Rating Board. In April 2016, Roblox VR for Oculus Rift was launched. At the time of release, more than ten million games were available in 3D. Around the same time, the secure chat feature was removed and replaced with a system based on a whitelist with a set of acceptable words for users under 13 and a blacklist for other users. In June of the same year, the company released a version compatible with Windows 10.
